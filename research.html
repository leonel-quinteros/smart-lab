Research

Our laboratory conducts world-class research in structural materials, multiscale mechanics, and computational methods. We bridge scales from atomic to macroscopic phenomena to solve real engineering problems.

1. Phase-Field Fracture Modeling

We develop micromechanics-based phase-field models to predict fracture behavior in composite materials, particularly CNT (carbon nanotube) composites. Phase-field methods provide a powerful framework for simulating complex fracture phenomena without explicit crack tracking. Our research focuses on electromechanical coupling, corrosion-driven degradation, and predictive integrity assessment of corroded structures.

Key applications: CNT composites, piezoresistive materials, corroded cables and infrastructure, damage prediction

2. Phononic Crystals & Band Gap Optimization

We investigate the design and optimization of phononic structures—materials engineered to control, direct, and manipulate acoustic and elastic waves. This includes sandwich panels with cellular truss cores and lattice structures. By optimizing geometry and topology, we can create phononic band gaps for vibration isolation, noise control, and dynamic damping applications.

Key applications: sandwich panels, cellular structures, vibration isolation, structural optimization, multidisciplinary design

3. Carbon Nanotube Composites & Smart Materials

CNT-based composites represent a frontier in multifunctional materials. We study the manufacturing, characterization, and mechanical properties of cementitious and polymeric composites reinforced with carbon nanotubes. These materials combine structural strength with electrical conductivity, enabling smart sensing and damage monitoring capabilities.

Key applications: conductive composites, self-sensing materials, structural health monitoring, conductive cement

4. Structural Health Monitoring & Damage Detection

We apply modal testing, impact hammer analysis, and sensor-based approaches to detect damage in composite structures and steel-concrete systems. This work bridges experimental mechanics with computational verification to develop reliable, practical damage detection methodologies for real infrastructure.

Key applications: damage assessment, experimental validation, SHM systems, composite and hybrid structures

4. Civil Engineering Applications

Our research directly addresses challenges in civil infrastructure: earthquake-resistant design, sustainable construction, smart materials, and structural health monitoring. We collaborate with practitioners to ensure our research translates to real-world impact.

Key focus areas: seismic analysis, retrofitting, sustainability, infrastructure resilience

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

We work with researchers across civil engineering, materials science, mechanical engineering, and physics. Our collaborations span national and international institutions, strengthening the quality and scope of our research.
