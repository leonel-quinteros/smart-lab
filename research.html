Research

Our laboratory conducts world-class research in structural materials, multiscale mechanics, and computational methods. We bridge scales from atomic to macroscopic phenomena to solve real engineering problems.

1. Structural Materials Analysis

We investigate the behavior, properties, and optimization of advanced materials used in civil engineering structures. This includes concrete, steel, composites, and innovative bio-based materials. Our work combines experimental characterization with computational modeling to understand material response under various environmental and loading conditions.

Key focus areas: durability, sustainability, fracture mechanics, microstructural analysis

2. Multiscale Mechanics

Multiscale mechanics is central to our research. We develop and apply methods to bridge different length and time scales—from nanoscale atomic interactions to macroscopic structural behavior. This approach enables us to predict material performance and design more efficient structures.

Key focus areas: homogenization theory, scale-bridging algorithms, computational efficiency

3. Computational Methods

We develop advanced numerical techniques including finite element methods, molecular dynamics simulations, and machine learning approaches for materials science and structural analysis. Our computational tools help accelerate research and enable exploration of design spaces that are infeasible experimentally.

Key focus areas: FEM, molecular dynamics, finite volume methods, inverse problems, data-driven approaches

4. Civil Engineering Applications

Our research directly addresses challenges in civil infrastructure: earthquake-resistant design, sustainable construction, smart materials, and structural health monitoring. We collaborate with practitioners to ensure our research translates to real-world impact.

Key focus areas: seismic analysis, retrofitting, sustainability, infrastructure resilience

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

We work with researchers across civil engineering, materials science, mechanical engineering, and physics. Our collaborations span national and international institutions, strengthening the quality and scope of our research.
