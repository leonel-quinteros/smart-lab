Research Focus

SM-Art Lab conducts cutting-edge research in computational mechanics and advanced materials. Our work spans four interconnected areas:

  • Phase-Field Fracture Modeling: Micromechanics-based models for CNT composites, piezoresistive materials, and corrosion-driven degradation in civil infrastructure.
  • Phononic Crystals & Band Gap Optimization: Design and optimization of acoustic metamaterials for vibration isolation, damping, and dynamic control in structural systems.
  • Carbon Nanotube Composites: Development of multifunctional CNT-based composites with self-sensing and conductive properties for smart infrastructure.
  • Structural Health Monitoring: Experimental and computational methods for damage detection and integrity assessment in composite and hybrid structures.

About SM-Art Lab

Based in the Civil Engineering Department of Universidad de Chile, SM-Art Lab (Structural Materials and Multiscale Mechanics Laboratory) is directed by Prof. Leonel Quinteros. We are committed to advancing knowledge through rigorous research, training the next generation of engineers and scientists, and developing computational tools that tackle real engineering challenges in materials and structures.
